# Staff Physicist I

My 5 years at Stable Laser Systems was a period of intense learning and carving out a career path. The flagship products of the company are bespoke, narrow linewidth, ultrastable frequency sources using IR lasers locked to Fabry-Perot cavities. Much of my initial work was focused on feed-forward and feedback laser frequency correction using approaches like analog PID and adaptive digital FIR filters. After proving I could handle complicated and abstract tasks, I was given near-complete ownership of the [Integrated Laser System (ILS)](https://stablelasers.com/products/integrated-1hz-stabilized-laser-systems/) electronics and software, Tzc characterization of [Fabry-Perot cavities](https://stablelasers.com/fabry-perot-cavities/), and assembly and testing of the [PDH signal units](https://stablelasers.com/products/pound-drever-hall-signal-unit/).

As with any employee in a very small company, we wore many hats. I was often tasked with making improvements to our test automation infrastructure, which required familiarizing myself with the Matlab code, the electronics involved (such as temperature controllers, spectrum analyzers, frequency generators), and the physical system under test. I frequently had to modify analog electronics, eventually taking over management of our entire electronics component inventory and assembly process. And when control code was not available for a given device or system, it typically fell to me to create a driver or GUI. I was the sole contributor and maintainer of an internal Python library for creating desktop and embedded GUIs, which may be seen on a [range of electronics products.](https://stablelasers.com/electronics/) On these embedded systems, the entire GUI is navigable and controllable with the [Grayhill 60AR Joystick](https://grayhill.com/products/joystick-controls/multifunction-joysticks/navcoder-multifunction-joystick/60ar/), for which I wrote the driver code and designed an interface circuit board.
