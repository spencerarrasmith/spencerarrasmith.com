# B.S. Electrical and Computer Engineering

After originally drifting toward a Computer Science major, I found my place during sophomore year in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department. While this indecision seemed harmless and even healthy at the time, as it allowed me to more broadly explore subjects like Spanish, Math, and Chinese, it would mean that my remaining two years would be densely packed with engineering courses to make up for lost time. Despite this, I remained active with clubs, intramural sports, and on-campus leadership opportunities.

Rice University offers a tremendous wealth of intellectual and kind professors, but the most valuable lesson for me was the value of forming and maintaining connections with fellow students - a lesson I have carried forward into my career. Rice University was also tremendously humbling, and taught me that learning is a lifelong assignment which will never be complete.
